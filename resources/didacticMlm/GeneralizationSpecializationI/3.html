3. Reflect about Inheritance

It might have been obvious that Car and Train have similar attributes and can be regarded as children of Vehicle. However, depending on what is being modeled it can also make sense to also add this inheritance to Horse. Since, the attributes of Vehicle are also applicable to it. Thus, adding all three classes as children of Vehicle is also a viable solution.

For example, if one would want to simply compare methods of transportation to find the fastest travelling option it might be appropriate to consider a horse a vehicle. However, in a scenario where one would maybe own and maintain cars and horses their requirements could be to different to inherit from the same class. Horses might need to have a vet, be taken outside regularly, or have their attributes like horse power change as they get older. Especially if they are not the only animals being maintained it might make more sense for them to be children of another class (e.g. animal or mammal) which would fit their requirements more closely.

The importan thing to note is that these decisions depent on the intended use and context. So, there is always more than one correct solution.
